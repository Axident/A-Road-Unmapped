Washing a semi truck is not something you want to do by hand. At the larger Schneider outposts, you could get a decent meal, shower, fuel up for free and get your rig washed and even repaired in a single stop. A repair wasn’t something I thought I needed when I pulled up to the wash.

“How big are you gonna let that crack get?” The attendant said, pointing at the small fracture in my windshield.

“It’s been there for weeks,” I shrugged, “I think that’s as bad as it’s going to get.”

“Nonsense,” he shook his head, “Head over to the drive through inspection after this.”

The inspection tunnel at this location was a full service operation. They checked the air pressure in the tires, changed the filter in the air dryer and replaced 2 amber light bulbs before I knew it was my turn.

“Take your shit off the dash!” someone yelled up from the side of my truck as he rapped on the door with his gloved hand.

“Oh right, thanks!” I said thinking that when they were done I’d leave my truck with them to replace the windshield.

But as I shuffled a few log books to the seat next to me, I realized that this was happening right now. They wheeled up a short cat walk in front of my grill and began running a small bladed tool around the massive wall of glass in front of me. I quickly pulled the rest of the items off the dashboard as they suctioned the entire sheet off the frame. He ran some sort of sealant around the border and stepped aside. As fast as the old windshield came off, another guy lined up a fresh one in its place.

“All set driver,” he nodded, “stay safe.”

And just like that, I was ready to head out for my next run.
