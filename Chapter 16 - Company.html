When other drivers were around, you were usually in pretty good company.

“You gotcher ears on, Jimmy Dean?”  a voice suddenly crackled over the cb as I filled out my log book at the end of my day.

The radio was silent for a moment before it responded, “yep.”

“Then who’s that in front of yer truck?”

Headlights snapped on from the truck next to me pouring across the asphalt on the trucks across the aisle. Soon another, then another. I felt obligated to add mine and flipped the switch on my dashboard. Now the row between our trucks was lit up like a sports arena, and the figure in question was shielding their eyes and waving down our lights. Next to them a small dog came out from peeing on the tires and stood close to the man's legs.

“Shit its just Eddie and his dog,” the guy apologised and lights began turning off.

As each truck turned off their headlights some of them left on their amber parking lights. It was a nice orange glow, and I thought I’d do the same. After a few minutes there was a knock on my door.

This was very unusual. in fact, nobody had ever knocked on my door before.

I rolled down my window and looked down to see a young woman in a skirt and unzipped hoodie. She was not wearing a shirt. “Whatcha in the mood for tonight, sweetheart,” she asked.

“Excuse me?”

“You lookin for a date or not?” She was clearly disappointed in my confusion.

“Uh no,” I felt terribly awkward, “no thanks”.

“Then loose the lights asshole,” she spun on her heels and walked to the next lit truck.

I watched in a bit of shock as she climbed into someone else's truck and the lights went off. Scanning the parking lot, I could see 2 other underdressed women who apparently worked this lot. At breakfast the next morning I would learn the term was “lot lizard.” This was not the last time I’d see this, but it was the last time I left my amber lights on.
