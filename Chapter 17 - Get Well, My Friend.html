I arrived back at my apartment at about 10am but it felt less like home than ever. I had made my tractor quite comfortable and was spending way more time there than here. Again the house seemed abandoned, except for a very expensive looking leather jacket hung on the back of a chair in the kitchen. It smelled heavy of smoke and alcohol.

After a few hours of television and Lucky Charms I heard Tim’s door open.

“Oh hey,” he said stumbling into the room, “good morning.”

I laughed, “good afternoon you dope, it's already 2:30”

“Oh damn, my bad” he laughed flopped into a seat next to me.

“Is that your jacket in the kitchen?”

“Yeah, you like it?”

“It kinda smells, man. Where’d you get it?”

“A bike shop on broadway.”

“Down by all the bars?”

Tim just shot me a look. He knew where I was going with this. I thought this was about to turn into a big fight, but the writing was on the wall. Tim moved out later that day, and I ended the lease on that apartment at the end of the month.

The road was my home now; more than it ever had been before. Somehow reducing my address to a P.O. box made me feel adrift at a whole new level. I’d need something to anchor me, or I was lost.
