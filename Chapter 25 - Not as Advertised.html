My next pickup was in New Jersey. I pulled up with an empty trailer and a big grin. I knew what I was bringing would be valuable to someone waiting here. Besides drivers, the thing that big trucking outfits never have enough of is empty trailers. Too often a load will be ready for pick up and a driver will be available for the job; but without an empty trailer, time and money start slipping away while the shipment sits idle on the dock.

I parked the empty in the first open spot and went inside to talk to my dispatcher. Before he assigned me my next load, I had an important question for him.

“Is there any way I could get a load to St. Paul?” I asked.

“You can only have one home city,” he said, “but it can be any city in America. Do you want it to be St. Paul?”

“Yes.” I knew I’d be giving up free meals and laundry in Green Bay, but I had a good feeling about this. “Yes, let's make it St. Paul.”

“Done. You’re next load is at your location and due in St. Paul 2 and ½ days from now.”

I hung up and went back to my cab to wait for the dashboard computer to light up with details. While I waited I plotted out my course. If I fudged the numbers a bit, I could be in St. Paul in 2 days, giving me a 3 day weekend with Michelle. I called her and gave her the good news. We made some plans as my trailer number and delivery location popped up on my computer for me to accept. I quickly said good night and confirmed the appointment.

Unfortunately, if I forced this journey into a 2 day ride - my overnight tomorrow was in the middle of nowhere. It had already been 2 days since I’d had a chance to shower and 2 more days was not going to make me a welcome guest at Michelle's. The place I was at listed a shower as one of it’s amenities. I grabbed my shower kit and a clean set of clothes and went inside.

“Hey, where’s the showers?” I asked the guy at the desk.

“What showers?” He said without looking up.

“Don’t you guys have showers? This listing says there are showers.”

“We used to have showers,” he said finally pulling his eyes up from his book. “Now we have a closet with a floor drain.”

“What?!? No, come on,” I couldn’t believe it. “Are you serious?”

“Sorry kid. No laundry, no towels, just a shower head sticking out of the wall in that little room back there.”

“I gotta have a shower,” I tried to explain, “I”m meeting a girl in 2 days and this is the last place I’m going to have a chance to take one.”

“There’s no hot water, no shower curtain, no towels or even a place to hang a towel.” He started, but could tell I was desperate, “but the room is all yours if you want it.”

“I’ll take it.”

The room was as bare as he described it: it didn’t even have a door. I tried to pile my things where they might not get soaked and took the coldest shower imaginable. Without any towel, I just brushed the water off my arms and body with swift strokes of my hands and forced my clothes on over my wet skin. I had to use the side view mirror in my truck to brush my hair, but when it was over I was relatively presentable.

Now to find my trailer. The computer had spit out my destination and directions accurately enough, but the trailer number listed for this trip was not parked with the rest. I had to go back inside for clarification.

“It’s out there kid,” the man at the front desk assured me, “look again.”

“I already triple checked, that trailer number is not in your yard.” I repeated.

“It has to be,” he said paging through listings on his computer, “The computer has shows it arrived 2 days ago.”

“Can you show me where it’s parked?” I tried to sound like I believed him.

With a heavy sigh, the man stood up and put on a jacket. We walked up and down the rows of trailers looking for something that matched the information on his clipboard. After some time we stopped in front of a trailer with a different number on it. “This must be it.”

“That number doesn’t match my instructions.” I pointed out.

“Yeah, but it doesn’t match my records either,” he paged through his clipboard. “This trailer isn’t supposed to be here,” he muttered and walked around to the doors in back. I followed him as he did a light inspection and checked the plastic seal on the lock. “The seal is still good, someone is expecting this delivery.”

“And you think they just got the trailer numbers mixed up?” I wanted to believe that was true, but mostly I just wanted a load to Minnesota.

“It’s happened before,” he made a note as he asked, “You want it?”

I nodded and signed the form.
