The next morning I made my obligatory weekly call to my dispatcher.

“How’s things going?” he asked.

“All good,” I gave the usual response. “Nothing to report.”

“Are you sure?” He was unusually persistent.

“Yeah, I had some issue with the pull handle but the shop says its fine.” I thought perhaps he got my repair request.

“You had it checked out at a Schneider lot?” It was good to make sure our own mechanics did the work. But if he didn’t know that yet, what was this about?

“It checked out I guess,” I didn’t want to make a big deal over the phone, “I’ll keep an eye on it.”

“Good idea.” He continued, “Gotta be safe - and drive save. Always be aware of everything around you.”

“Of course.” This was getting weird.

“And you’d tell me if anything happened right?” He let that question hang in the cold morning air.

I was scrambling to imagine where he might be going with this. My mind landed on the driver who tried to say I caused an accident. “Hey if this is about the near miss in New York, that's all it was. I think he was going to try some insurance scam thing but I never hit that guy.”

“Well that's good,” he sounded confused, “but I’m wondering about the fence in New Jersey. “

“Oh man, I’ll never forget that drop off, “ I explained, “Scary as hell. I couldn’t find anyone on site and the only spot left was against that rickety old fence. Honestly, I”m pretty sure it was broken before I got there.”

“Well they have you on camera backing into it,” he was obviously disappointed, “so Schneider’s buying them a new one.”

“Seriously?” I couldn’t believe it, “That’s messed up. I was just trying to make a delivery without getting mugged.”

“It’s ok”, he said, “We’ve heard bad things from other drivers about that location. I think we’re done with them anyway. But you need to tell me when things like this happen, alright?”

“Sure, I”m sorry. I wasn’t even sure I hit that fence,” I started to explain.

“Don’t worry about it. Listen, I’ve got some good news.” He suddenly changed tone, “there’s a local route in St. Paul we need covered if you want it.”

“What do you mean, local route?” This wasn’t a term I’d heard before.

“It’s a daily run inside the metro area, you’d be home every night.” He had a catch though, “It’s just temporary until Richard gets back from medical leave.”

“Are you kidding? That sounds great! How long?”

“A few months tops.” He started typing something on his computer, “So you’ll take it?”

“Totally! When does it start?” I was looking forward to something a bit less crazy than the road I’d been seeing.

“As soon as I can get you back to St. Paul,” he paused, “sound good?”

“Excellent!” I thanked him and hung up the phone. This was going to be so cool, I’d get to see Michelle every day! She’s going to be so surprised!
