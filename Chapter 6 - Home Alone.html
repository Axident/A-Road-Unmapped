After 2 weeks on the road, Schneider makes sure you get to spend a weekend in whatever city you’ve chosen as your home. Not that it feels like home anymore. Parking my truck at HQ and hopping into my ‘89 Beretta felt bizarre. I kept reaching for the clutch and making wide right turns all the way to my house in the dark. The place looked abandoned as I pulled into the driveway; no lights on at all. I guess I never really thought my unsteady relationship with Emily would survive long with me on the road for 2 weeks at a time, but her sudden silent disappearance was hard to take. Her friends assured me she was ok, but she didn’t want to see me again. I was surprised she didn’t even leave me a note.

My roommate Tim was also curiously absent, but showed up the next day for a shower and clean set of clothes. “Holy shit, you’re back!” He said fumbling with his key in the front door as he came in.

“Yeah, feels weird!” I said getting up off the couch. “I need to get out and do something.”

“Lemme clean up and we’ll figure something out,” he said on his way to the bathroom.

I called the theater at the mall to see what was playing. The pre-recorded message listed all the shows and times for the day. We decided to see a sci-fi called “The Matrix”. The movie did pretty well in theaters, but for me it was a real game changer. If it was possible to film something like this, anything you could imagine could be made into a movie. That thought would help me through the boredom of the endless road that lie ahead. There were of course, events that took my full concentration.
