Whenever I hear the song “America the Beautiful”, I can’t help but remember my drive through upstate New York. The road through the mountains and up the coast towards Maine is truly a sight to behold. The scenery is fantastic, the air smells different, and the harbor towns along the coast fascinated me. I’d never seen the ocean first hand before and the sight of it was literally breathtaking.

It was nearly lunch time, so I started looking for a place to pull over and give Michelle a call. She would love to hear about this part of the trip. Once I stopped, I pulled out my phone and saw the date on the screen. I couldn’t believe it, I almost forgot to call my brother!

“Hey bro,” I said as he picked up, “Happy Birthday!”

“Oh hey! Thanks!” he answered, “Are you out on the road?”

“Yeah, guess where?” I laughed, “never mind, you’ll never guess. I”m in New Hampshire!”

“Really? What’s it like?” he asked.

“So friggin beautiful, you wouldn't believe it.” I went into a brief description of the landscape.

“Cool! Where are you headed?”

“My next load is in Maine” I explained.

“You’ll have to tell me how the lobster is,” we laughed and let each other go, promising to talk more often.

Growing up in Wisconsin, lobster was about the only thing we knew that Maine had going for it. I was discovering that the state had plenty to offer, but there's a good reason people think lobster when they think about Maine. You can get it anywhere. Less than 30 minutes after hanging up with my brother I passed a sign for a fast food drive through that said “Fresh lobster. Truckers welcome.”

This had to be a joke, but I needed to be sure. I pulled my truck around back where there was room for my trailer and went inside.

“Do you guys actually have lobster?” I asked the teenager behind the register.

“I can get you one if you want, will you be travelling far with it?” he asked.

This was not the response I was expecting. “Can I eat it here?” I asked.

“It’s a live lobster, sir.”

“Ha, ha!” I laughed, “I guess not then! You guys don’t cook them here?”

“Not anymore. But we still run and get them from the store across the street if you want one.”

“Wow, that’s funny,” I was still laughing, “no thanks.”

“Can I get you something else?”

“I’m good, thanks anyway,” I said smiling and turned to the door.

I did hear some other truckers on the CB radio later mentioning that you could get lobster at the drive-throughs in Maine, but I never got clarification on whether you could get one cooked.
