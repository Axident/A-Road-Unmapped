“Hi, Mom!” I said opening the front door to my parents house, “I’m home!”

“Hi sweetie,” she threw her arms around me and kissed me on the cheek, “come in, tell me all about it!”

Dinner was probably some typical midwestern meat and potatoes kind of thing, but nothing beats a home cooked meal after all those days on the road. I explained the safer PG rated version of my job and how beautiful the scenery was. Of course Mom mostly wanted to know how safe driving a semi-truck really was. After dinner I sat in an easy chair in the family room by the tv next to my father. After some discussion about how we expect the Packers to do this year, the phone rang.

“Patrick,” my Mom called, “it’s for you!”

“What?” This was truly unexpected, “Who even knows I'm here? Is it work?”

“It’s a girl,” Mom handed me the phone.

“Hello?” my head searching for a name of anyone I might have told that I would be in town.

“Hi, this is Michelle” the sweetest voice I could’ve imagined started, “We met in St. Paul...”

“Oh wow! Yes!” I needed to tone it down a bit, “Yeah, you’re Laura’s friend.”

Smooth, I thought. Good job telling her who she’s friends with - she might not have known otherwise. Fortunately, Michelle was really good at talking so I tried to be a good listener. After all the bizarre and shocking opinions pouring out of my CB radio for countless hours on the road, it was refreshing to talk with someone intelligent over the phone. We chatted for a while and eventually said good night. I hung up reasonably confident that it would be worth my while to invest in a cell phone.
