Later that week I’d find myself in very different weather. The still night air had cooled considerably and a thick heavy fog fell over the freeway. Stopping was not an option, I had too far to go yet tonight to just call it quits. I resigned myself to simply put on my 4 ways, drive slow, and keep myself awake listening to the cb radio.

“I never did upgrade my lights,” a driver was saying, “do you think it’s worth it?”

“Lemme tell ya,” an authoritative voice came over the air waves, “I can tell you exactly what you get for your money.”

His voice sounded as though he should be doing commercials for the Marines or something. Nobody was going to interrupt him, surely whatever he said next could be taken as gospel.

“I used to wonder why people had those fancy yellow fog lights installed,” he continued. “But every driver I talked do failed to describe any real benefit.”

I turned up my cb and listened to his story as we all rolled slowly onward through the heavy low clouds.

“I’d forget about it for a while, and then of course the next foggy day would make me think about it again. Eventually I needed to find out for myself and I pulled into the shop and had the newest golden bulbs professionally installed right in the bumper. I waited weeks for any sign of fog. More than a month went buy until finally I had the chance to fick that expensive little toggle switch and see if they made a difference. You want to know what that money gets you after you finally get a chance to use ‘em?”

We all waited for a beat as though the meaning of life itself were about to be revealed.

He clicks the talk button on his cb and abruptly ends the story: “Yellow fog.”
