Creating a science fiction story in my head was a great way to pass the time on long boring trips like this. It did nothing however, to fight the worst trick my mind had learned to play on the road: doubt. Was that load you just picked up properly balanced? Is that truck stop still ahead, or was it closed somewhere behind me? Or in this case, are these driving instructions accurate?

I’d been driving for hours down an unlit county highway with no sign of life wondering if I’d missed a turn when I finally saw some light ahead. It was a cop car with its turn signal on in front of a lonely farm house. There was a bright yard light in front of the house and I could see an officer standing next to the car by the road.

I let off the gas and slowed to a crawl with my 4 way lights on. I rolled down the window to ask for directions as I approached the car.

“Excuse me officer,” I called down from my rig, “Is this way to -”

The officer was leaning on his car, he turned to face me and yelled “What?”  As he turned I could see the front of his uniform was wide open and on the white t-shirt underneath there was large red spot covering most of his chest.

“Are you ok?” I had no idea what I would do if he said no.

“Yeah, yeah - keep driving!” He waved me on and turned back to his car to grab something through the window.

I did not stick around to see what happened next.
